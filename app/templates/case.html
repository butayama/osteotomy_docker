Objective

To report a surgical correction of a multiplanar deformity of the right radius in a pony using a single cut osteotomy. Study design: Case report.

Animals

A nine-week old male Shetland pony foal.

Methods

The foal was presented with a multiplanar deformity of the right radius. The deformity was corrected using a single cut osteotomy based on a 3-d pre surgical planning and using the mathematical approach from Sangeorzan et al. for calculation of the cutting plane. After the osteotomy the bone was stabilized with 4.5mm LCP double plating.

Results

The follow up showed a satisfactory outcome.

Conclusion

The single cut osteotomy is a straightforward option for correction of a multiplanar deformity of long bones in horses. The surgical procedure requires an intensive pre surgical planning.

Clinical significance

The first reported case of a single cut osteotomy for correction of a multiplanar deformity of a long bone of a horse. The tool developed by the authors will potentially facilitate the pre surgical planning for upcoming cases.
